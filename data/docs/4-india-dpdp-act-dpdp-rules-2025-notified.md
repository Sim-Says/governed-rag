## 4. India: DPDP Act + DPDP Rules, 2025 (Notified)

**What it is:** India's data protection law, enacted Aug 2023. **DPDP Rules, 2025 have been officially notified** — transforming the Act from a legislative framework into an enforceable compliance regime. Published by MeitY in Nov 2025. Implementation is phased through May 2027.

### Key Provisions
- **Consent-based processing:** Free, specific, informed, unambiguous consent. Clear notice in simple language.
- **Data fiduciary / Data principal:** Fiduciary = controller/processor; Principal = the individual.
- **Purpose limitation:** Process only for the consented purpose.
- **Data minimization:** Collect only what's necessary.
- **Storage limitation:** Retain only as long as needed; then delete.
- **Rights of data principal:** Access, correction, erasure, grievance redressal, nominate, withdraw consent.
- **Significant Data Fiduciaries (SDFs):** Must appoint DPO, independent Data Auditor, conduct DPIA, breach reporting.
- **Cross-border transfer:** To countries not blacklisted by government (white-list approach).
- **Penalties:** Up to ₹250 crore (~$30M) per instance. Adjudication by Data Protection Board (DPB).
- **Children's data:** Verifiable parental consent required for users under 18; no targeted advertising/behavioral tracking of children.

### Implementation Timeline (3 Phases)
| Phase | Date | What Activates |
|---|---|---|
| **Phase 1** | Effective immediately (Nov 2025) | Data Protection Board of India established and operational |
| **Phase 2** | November 13, 2026 | Consent Managers framework — individuals can register and manage data preferences |
| **Phase 3** | May 13, 2027 | Full substantive compliance — all obligations on data fiduciaries, breach reporting, children's data, cross-border restrictions |
