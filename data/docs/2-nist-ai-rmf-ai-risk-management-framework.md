## 2. NIST AI RMF (AI Risk Management Framework)

**What it is:** Voluntary framework from U.S. NIST (Jan 2023, GenAI Profile added July 2024). De facto U.S. governance baseline, referenced in executive orders. Not regulation — but widely adopted.

### The 4 Functions

| Function | Core Question | Key Actions |
|----------|---------------|-------------|
| **GOVERN** | Who is accountable and how? | Policies, roles, responsibilities, risk tolerance, governance committee |
| **MAP** | What risks exist in this context? | Context identification, risk profiling, impact assessment, use-case classification |
| **MEASURE** | How do we quantify/track risks? | Performance metrics, robustness testing, red-teaming, bias evaluation, drift detection |
| **MANAGE** | What do we do about identified risks? | Mitigation controls, incident response, monitoring, documentation, third-party risk |

### Trustworthy AI Characteristics
Valid, reliable, safe, secure, accountable, explainable, privacy-enhanced, fair/bias-managed.

### Generative AI Profile (NIST AI 600-1, July 2024)
Adds GAI-specific risks: hallucination, data provenance, content provenance (C2PA/watermarking), dual-use, dangerous imagery, information integrity, bias amplification, confabulation, IP.
