## 1. EU AI Act

**What it is:** World's first comprehensive horizontal AI regulation (passed 2024, phased through 2026–2027). Regulates AI systems placed on the EU market based on risk level.

### Risk Tiers

| Tier | Examples | Obligation | Penalty |
|------|----------|------------|---------|
| **Unacceptable** (banned) | Social scoring, real-time biometric ID, manipulative AI | Prohibited | €35M or 7% global turnover |
| **High-risk** | Recruitment, credit scoring, medical devices, critical infrastructure, law enforcement, migration | Full compliance regime | €15M or 3% turnover |
| **Limited risk** | Chatbots, deepfakes, emotion recognition | Transparency — users must know they're interacting with AI | — |
| **Minimal risk** | Spam filters, video games, inventory | No obligations beyond voluntary codes | — |

### High-Risk Obligations (the ones that show up in JDs)
- Risk management system (lifecycle, continuous)
- Data governance — training data quality, representativeness
- Technical documentation & record-keeping (audit trail)
- Transparency & user instructions-for-use
- **Human oversight** (mandatory, "effective," can't be automated away)
- Accuracy, robustness, cybersecurity
- Conformity assessment + CE marking before market placement
- Post-market monitoring (report serious incidents)
- Registration in EU database

### Timeline
- Feb 2025: Prohibitions on unacceptable-risk AI took effect
- Aug 2025: GPAI model obligations + governance rules
- **Aug 2026: High-risk AI system obligations take effect** ← the big one
- Aug 2027: Obligations for high-risk systems embedded in regulated products
