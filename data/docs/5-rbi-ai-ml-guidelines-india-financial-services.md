## 5. RBI AI/ML Guidelines (India — Financial Services)

**What it is:** RBI has released the **FREE-AI Framework** (Framework for Responsible and Ethical Enablement of AI) — the first formal AI governance framework for India's financial sector. Combined with existing circulars on digital lending, model risk, and data localization.

### RBI FREE-AI Framework (2025)

**6 Thematic Pillars:**
1. **Infrastructure** — Expanding access to high-quality data and shared computational resources
2. **Policy & Regulation** — Agile regulatory approaches that address model risks
3. **Capacity** — Investing in employee skills and competence regarding new technologies
4. **Governance** — Clear institutional accountability for AI-based outcomes
5. **Protection** — Mitigating algorithmic bias and ensuring strict consumer safeguards
6. **Assurance** — Continuous monitoring and independent third-party audits

**7 Guiding Principles ("Sutras") for Regulated Entities:**
1. **Trust is the Foundation** — AI systems must be transparent, verifiable, and reliable
2. **People First** — Technology should augment—not replace—human judgment
3. **Innovation over Restraint** — Encouraging secure experimentation without abandoning regulatory standards
4. **Fairness and Equity** — Outcomes must be unbiased and non-discriminatory across all user groups
5. **Accountability** — The institution deploying the AI bears ultimate responsibility for its decisions and impacts
6. **Understandable by Design** — Models must be interpretable for both users and regulators (limiting opaque "black-box" decisions)
7. **Safety, Resilience & Sustainability** — Systems must be secure, operationally stable, and adaptable over time

### Other Key Instruments
- **Regulatory Principles for Management of Model Risks in Credit** — Model development, validation, deployment monitoring, explainability, fairness, bias testing for credit models. Aligns with SR 11-7 (U.S. Fed model risk) principles.
- **IT Governance / Outsourcing:** Board-level oversight of IT and AI. Outsourcing AI to third parties doesn't outsource accountability.
- **Cyber Security Framework:** Security testing, incident reporting (within 6 hours), data localization.
- **Digital Lending Guidelines (2022):** Algorithmic lending must be transparent; borrower must be informed of algorithmic decisioning; grievance redressal mandatory. Directly applies to AI-based credit scoring.
- **Data Localization (RBI Act Section 10(2)):** Payments data must stay in India. AI models processing payment data need domestic infrastructure.
- **SEBI Consultation Paper on AI/ML (June 2025):** Capital markets regulator also consulting on AI/ML guidelines for market infrastructure.
